About VocaliD

VocaliD (for vocal identity) is dedicated to making synthetic voices more human. Leveraging years of research and development, we’re designing customized voices that empower our users to be heard.

If you're familiar with Stephen Hawking’s iconic voice, you may be shocked to learn that many people around the world — young and old, male and female — speak with the exact same voice.

There are tens of millions worldwide who rely on computerized voices to express themselves. Yet, many of them speak identically.

Add to that hundreds of millions who use text-to-speech technology everyday for things like smartphone assistants, GPS navigation, and screen readers — and the possibilities for what a unique VocaliD™ voice can offer become limitless.

Rupal Patel

Rupal founded VocaliD to give the gift of voice to all those who need and want a voice of their own. Her training as a speech clinician and scientist led to her initial insight to combine the vocalizations of recipients with the speech of healthy donors. After years of research and experimentation, she is now committed to bringing this innovation to market and in doing so re-envisioning speech prostheses as custom crafted enhancements that make communication more human.

Rupal is a tenured professor at Northeastern University with joint appointments in Communication Science and Disorders and Computer Science as well as numerous adjunct and affiliated appointments at Harvard, MIT, University of Massachusetts and Yale University. She founded and directs the Communication Analysis and Design Laboratory and the Center for Speech Science and Technology. A native of Canada, Rupal received her BS from University of Calgary and her Masters and Ph.D from the University of Toronto. She then completed her post-doctoral studies at Massachusetts Institute of Technology where she worked with Professor Kenneth Stevens, a pre-eminent speech scientist. Rupal worked as a speech clinician prior to her doctoral studies which is where she became fascinated by the application of speech technologies to those with communication disorders. Patel has over 50 peer reviewed publications in the areas of speech motor control and assistive communication technology and has raised over $5 million in research funding through the National Science Foundation, various institutes within NIH (NIDCD, NICHD, NIBIB), and private foundations.

Geoff Meltzner

Geoff has deep expertise in speech signal processing and a passion for designing speech technologies that reimagine new ways to communicate. He has developed speech prostheses for alaryngeal speakers and recently led a multimillion dollar, interdisciplinary effort to build the first-ever mobile silent speech recognition system. Given his background in speech modification and experience managing large scale engineering efforts, he will help scale VocaliD’s personalized synthesis efforts.

Geoff is the Section Lead of the Biological, Audio, and Speech Signal group at BAE Systems. He received his B.S. in engineering from Harvey Mudd College, his S.M. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from MIT and his Ph.D. in Speech and Hearing Biosciences and Technology also from MIT. His graduate work, co-supervised by Professor Kenneth Stevens and Robert Hillman, focused on improving the quality of alaryngeal speech. He has over 15 years of industry and research experience in speech processing, speech coding, and biological signal processing. During the course of his career, Geoff has won, led, and managed multiple multi-million dollar DARPA efforts to develop technologies ranging from gait-based biometrics, to brain control of prosthetic arms, and most notably a practical portable silent-speech recognition system that can be used to improve the communication of people with severe speech disorders.

Michael Suen

Michael is an interaction designer and strategist who has worked with startups, universities, foundations, and non-profit organizations to create visual identities, branding campaigns, and user interfaces. His interest in designing playful user experiences for the social web led to his current role at VocaliD, where he defines strategy for audience engagement. Michael began his career as a journalist and copywriter, before joining the MIT non-profit spin-off Learning Games Network in 2012 as a digital producer. There, he produced games, websites, and branding initiatives with partners that include the UN Alliance of Civilizations and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. In 2013, Michael's team was recognized for their work on the ethics game Quandary, named Game of the Year at the Games For Change Festival.

Michael is also the co-founder of Switch, a platform to search and review alternative educational resources. A passionate advocate of interdisciplinary learning in the liberal arts, he has spoken about preparing humanities graduates for careers at events organized by the Humanities, Arts, Science, and Technology Alliance and Collaboratory (HASTAC) and the National Institute for Technology in Liberal Education (NITLE).

Michael’s work has been featured in TIME.com, TechCrunch, Wired, Voice of America, and Kill Screen. He has also presented on digital media and learning at MIT, Brown University, Bryn Mawr College, and the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Michael graduated with highest honors in English & American Literatures from Middlebury College in 2011.

Julia Wilson

Julia is a designer and photographer whose work has been featured in Gallery 360 and CauseInform. Her design portfolio includes KIND Healthy Snacks, Scout Design, Evolutionary IT and Adobe. She is passionate about working with socially conscious companies and has experience with non-profit campaign development. At VocaliD, she is applying her skills in web design, animation, photography and packaging design to create a unique identity for the company.

Julia is a senior at Northeastern University pursing a B.F.A. in Graphic Design and a B.A. in Journalism in the College of Arts, Media and Design. Through her co-op opportunities and freelance work she has honed her brand development and web design skills. Her projects have included identity development, web design and coding, animation, photography, packaging and information design. Julia also has a special interest in communication through design and photography. She has studied photography in Cuba, focusing on body language within culture barriers. She also developed an anti-drug campaign, which has been featured in CauseInform and OnlyInBoston.

Grace Bernicker

As a singer/songwriter and active voice user Grace understands the importance of having your own unique vocal identity. As a Communications Intern she will be responsible for driving strategic communications, overseeing social media efforts, and working with partnership initiatives.

Grace is a 2013 graduate of Temple University where she received her B.A. in Communications with a focus in Broadcast, Telecommunications and Mass Media. As a singer/songwriter she released her debut singles in April 2012. All songs were produced and co-written by her co-writer/producer Kenny Lamb (N’SYNC/Justin Timberlake/Rhett Akins) with engineering and mixing by Chris Rowe (Taylor Swift/Jewel). Since her release in April 2012 Grace has played over 80+ shows up & down the east coast, including numerous venues in Nashville, TN. Some of her favorite venues to date include The Bluebird Cafe, World Cafe Live & performing pre-game on the field at the Wilmington Blue Rocks Stadium. As an independent artist she has single handedly been responsible for all marketing, show booking and project planning. Grace went from #37 on the ReverbNation Country chart for Philadelphia to #1 in 9 months. As a vocalist, Grace studies under the tutelage of Badiene Magaziner at Badiene Magaziner Vocal Studio in New York City.
